Privacy policy

How Pyin Mal handles data in this contest build.

This project is a front-end only prototype for a design contest. That means there is no backend, no database, and we are not storing any personal data from visitors.

Important

  • • We do not store or transmit any camera, image, or form data in this demo.
  • • There are no cookies, analytics scripts, or trackers enabled right now.
  • • Future real-world versions may add analytics and storage, which will require an updated policy.

1. Scope of this policy

This privacy policy applies to the contest version of the Pyin Mal styling platform. The goal of this build is to demonstrate UI/UX, not to handle real customer data.

Any references to “we”, “our”, or “Pyin Mal” in this document refer to the project team building this prototype.

2. What data we collect in this demo

In this contest version, the website runs entirely in your browser. We do not send any of the following to a server:

  • Camera feed or uploaded photos from the Face Scan page.
  • Any text typed into forms (Contact, FAQ teaser, pricing interest forms).
  • Any selections made in filters, generators, or Saved Fashion.

All interactions stay on your device and are cleared when you refresh or close the page.

3. Face scan & MediaPipe (future state)

The Face Scan page is currently a UI shell that simulates the flow but does not actually run MediaPipe. In a real-world implementation we plan to:

  • Process camera and image data locally in your browser using MediaPipe Face Mesh.
  • Avoid sending raw face images to our servers unless you explicitly agree.
  • Optionally store anonymised measurements (for example, face shape classification) to improve recommendations.

Before launching a real service, we would update this policy with clear details and consent options.

4. Cookies and analytics

Right now, this prototype does not use any cookies, analytics tools, or third-party trackers such as Google Analytics, Meta Pixel, or session replay services.

For a future production version, we may integrate:

  • Basic analytics to understand which pages are visited the most.
  • Conversion tracking to see which styling plans are popular.

If we add these, we will:

  • Provide a cookie banner or settings panel.
  • Explain what data is collected and why.
  • Offer an easy way to opt out of non-essential cookies.

5. Forms & contact

The contact and question forms in this contest build are purely visual. Submissions are not actually sent anywhere.

In a real deployment, we would connect these forms to an email inbox or CRM. At that point we would explain:

  • What fields are required (name, email, styling goals, etc.).
  • How long we keep messages.
  • How you can request deletion of your information.

6. Your rights

Because this prototype doesn’t store personal data, there is nothing for us to access, export, or delete on your behalf.

For a real product, you should expect rights such as:

  • Access to the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction of inaccurate data.
  • Deletion of your data on request, where applicable.

7. Contact

If you have questions about how this prototype handles privacy, or if you want help adapting this policy for a real deployment, you can reach the project team via the Contact page.

This privacy policy is a sample document created for a design contest and is not legal advice. For a production deployment, please have a lawyer review and localize this text.
